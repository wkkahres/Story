The Mail"

"It was a dark and stormy night.

The Dover mail was due at the usual hour; but it was late,
and the rocket driver had not yet come in.

The weather was so cold and spacey that the air was full of stars,
and the ground was as slippery as ice.
As the rocket rumbled through the stars, the engines seemed to echo louder and louder in the silence,
and the sound of the nothingness in the void was the only noise to be heard
